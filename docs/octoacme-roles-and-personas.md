# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Process Owner

### Role Summary
Process Owners oversee the health and evolution of defined project management processes. They ensure processes remain current, are consistently applied across teams, and address documented gaps or inefficiencies. They work collaboratively with stakeholders to identify and implement process improvements.

### Responsibilities
- Monitor process effectiveness and identify improvement opportunities
- Review and update process documentation based on team feedback
- Ensure all stakeholders understand and adhere to established processes
- Facilitate process improvement initiatives and gather feedback
- Address documented gaps or bottlenecks in project workflows
- Coordinate training and onboarding on process changes

### Goals
- Maintain clear, accessible, and up-to-date process documentation
- Foster consistent process adoption across all teams
- Continuously improve efficiency and reduce process friction
- Ensure accountability and clarity in role responsibilities

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Works with **Project Managers** to validate process applicability and gather feedback
- Collaborates with **Developers** and **Product Managers** on practical implementation
- Partners with **Agile Coach** on process evolution and training needs
- Engages **Quality Lead** to ensure processes support quality outcomes

### Typical Communication
- Process review meetings and retrospectives
- Documentation updates and version control
- Stakeholder feedback sessions and improvement proposals
- Cross-functional process working groups

---

## Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Agile Coaches guide teams in adopting and improving agile methodologies and practices. They work across organizational boundaries to help teams increase velocity, improve collaboration, and remove impediments. They partner with Project Managers and individual contributors to drive continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Coach teams on agile ceremonies and best practices
- Help teams identify and remove workflow impediments
- Facilitate adoption of agile tools and techniques
- Mentor team leads and managers on agile principles
- Identify process bottlenecks and recommend improvements
- Support retrospectives and facilitate continuous improvement

### Goals
- Increase team velocity and delivery predictability
- Improve team collaboration and communication
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Ensure agile practices are tailored to team contexts

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Partners with **Project Managers** to align agile practices with delivery timelines
- Supports **Developers** in improving workflow and reducing technical debt
- Collaborates with **Process Owner** on process improvements and adoption
- Works with **Quality Lead** to integrate quality practices into agile workflows

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and retrospective facilitation
- One-on-one coaching sessions
- Training workshops and skill-building sessions
- Process improvement recommendations and team feedback

---

## Stakeholder Liaison

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Liaisons act as communication bridges between delivery teams and key stakeholders. They ensure requirements are clearly understood, feedback is collected, and stakeholder concerns are elevated appropriately. They foster alignment and transparency across organizational boundaries.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and communicate stakeholder requirements and feedback to delivery teams
- Provide regular updates on project status and progress to stakeholders
- Escalate risks, blockers, or concerns that impact stakeholder interests
- Facilitate stakeholder review sessions and acceptance signoffs
- Identify and manage stakeholder expectations
- Document and track stakeholder decisions and approvals

### Goals
- Ensure clear, timely communication between teams and stakeholders
- Maintain stakeholder alignment and satisfaction
- Reduce miscommunication and rework due to unmet expectations
- Enable faster decision-making through effective information flow

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Works with **Project Managers** to coordinate status and escalations
- Engages **Product Managers** on requirement clarification and prioritization
- Collaborates with **Developers** to communicate technical feasibility concerns
- Partners with **Quality Lead** on acceptance criteria and quality standards

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder briefings and status updates
- Requirement review meetings and feedback sessions
- Executive summaries and decision documentation
- Escalation and risk communication channels

---

## Quality Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Leads own the quality assurance strategy and protocols for project deliverables. They work with development and project management teams to define quality standards, establish testing strategies, and ensure deliverables meet acceptance criteria before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define quality standards and acceptance criteria for deliverables
- Develop and maintain testing strategies and test plans
- Coordinate testing activities and quality assurance efforts
- Identify quality risks and propose mitigation strategies
- Report on quality metrics and test coverage
- Work with teams to address quality issues and prevent defects

### Goals
- Ensure deliverables meet defined quality standards
- Reduce post-release defects and rework
- Maintain high test coverage and observability
- Enable confident release decisions through quality data

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Partners with **Developers** on test strategy and quality expectations
- Collaborates with **Product Managers** to understand acceptance criteria
- Works with **Project Managers** to integrate quality checkpoints into timelines
- Supports **Process Owner** in defining quality-related process standards

### Typical Communication
- Test planning and quality review meetings
- Quality metrics reports and dashboards
- Defect tracking and resolution discussions
- Release readiness assessments
